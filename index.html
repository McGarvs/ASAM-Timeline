1492

Christopher Columbus finds the Americas while in search of a western passage to Asia.

1565

Two centuries of trans-pacific trade beings with Spanish Galleons brining Asian goods and peoples to the Americas. Many of these first Asian immigrants come as slaves or as sailors forsaking a dreadful return voyage.

1607

Jamestown, the first permanent English Settlement in the United States, is founded. British colonies flourish with the establishment of Indentured servitude and the migration of many indentured European immigrants.

December 16, 1773

Americans dump 342 chests of tea from East India Company ships to protest British control over colonial trade and taxation.

February 22, 1784

The Empress of China departs from New York as the first American ship to trade with China. It returns laden with numerous luxury Chinese goods such as silks and porcelains.

1849

The California Gold Rush begins, drawing thousands of Chinese Immigrants to California and the West Coast of the United States. Chinese miners are seen as competitors and face violence from White Vigilante mobs and legal discrimination.

1853

The United States sends Commodore Perry to end Japan's Closed Door policy with gunboat diplomacy. The United States successfully negotiates an unequal treaty and starts a modernization process in Japan that leads to the latter Meiji Restoration.

1871

The United States sends naval forces against Korea in a show of Gunboat Diplomacy with hopes of recreating their previous success in Japan with Perry's Expedition.

October 24, 1871

The Largest Mass Lynching in United States history occurs in Los Angeles, where 17 Chinese are lynched by a massive mob following the shooting of a white policeman.
